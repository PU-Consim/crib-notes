Crib Note - France ’40

This crib note does not include all the rules. The missing parts should be inferred using the general rules I assume.

Terrain

  • Marsh River is a Major River.
  • Marsh around a minor River upgrades the hexside to a Major River hexside.

Unit Type

4. Stacking

  • Can stack up to 7 Stacking Points (SPs).
  • Units of the same division is 3- SPs.
  • Every stack is allowed to have one tank unit with 2- SP even if exceeding the stacking limit.

5. ZOC

  • HQs and static Fort units do not exert ZOC (white border lines).
  • Do not extend to All-sea/Tital River hexsides.
  • Entering EZOC: Stop upon entering. No extra cost.
  • Leaving EZOC: +2MPs. May move directly from EZOC to another EZOC unless crossing ZOC Bond.
  • Losing ZOCs: [In entry hex / under Train / Full Retreat]

6. ZOC Bond

  • Eligibility: Good Order 2+ DEF STR. Can form a ZOC Hexside Bond with a friendly map edge.
  • Restriction: Cannot extend [through a City Hex / across an All-Sea Tidal River hexside / across two Major river hexsides]. When determining if [City Hex / Major River] breaks, push the Hexside Bond one side; may not be pushed into a enemy unit.

7. Movement

  • Extended Movement increase MA. May not move adj to, but may begin adj from enemy unit.
  • All Units and HQs can move 2- hexes by Tactical Movement (TAC MOV).
  • Pontoon Bridges exist at Major River hexside where both sides are occupied by friendly Combat Units; even [one unit is moving / during AAC / Disrupted / in Full Retreat]. A unit forming it may move after providing other units crossings.
  • Crossing Unbrided Major Rivers: Must start adj to unless Pontoon.
  • Units may move normally after crossing rivers.

7.7 Automatic DS

  • Occurs when adj units makes 10-1 (including Tank and TQ Shift).
  • Units not participating can move through the vacated hexes.
  • Procedure: AutoDS on attacking units → cannot move further that Movement Phase → during the next Combat Phase, cannot participate regular combat, but can AAC anytime → remove AutoDS.
  • One stack in each Automatic DS attack may conduct BRK.

7.6 Allied Rail Movement

  • Entraining: Good Order units can entrain at [Town / City] 4+ hexes from German units; may move 2- hexes to reach.
  • Rail Movement: During movement phase, 15- rail hexes, 3- units per turn; must be [reinforcements / entrained in a previous turn]. Hexes traversed must be 4+ hexes from German [unit / controlled city]. May not end stacked with other Rail Moved Units.
  • Detraining: Beginning Allied Movement Phase, replace Train by Disrupted. May detrain in any rail hex. Must detrain if force to retreat or find itself 4- hexes of an enemy unit at the start of its move.

8~10. Combat

  • Need not pre-declare all. There exist 8-1 and 9-1 columns identical to 7-1.
  • Maximum STR: 20 Defense after halving and doubling. 40 Attack before halving.
  • Allied Cooperation: French, British and Belgian units may stack and defend together, but may not attack together.
  • Selecting Step losses: Infrantry Remnants are the last steps to be lost. A full strength unit must be selected first if German Panzer or Motorized divisions stack together.

Halving & Doubling

  • Halving
    • Applied only once per combat.
    • Applies to individual unit and round up (Exception: 1-STR units are added up and then halved).
    • ATK STR when [OOS / across Major River or Maginot Line / out of a Restricted Hex].
    • DEF STR when Disrupted.
  • Doubling
    • Applied only once per combat.
    • DEF STR if all attackers [across River / out of a Restricted Hex]

Combat Shifts

  • Tank, Heavy Tank (always): DEF terrain permitting
    • ATK [across River / out of a Restricted Hex] cannot provide but negate defender’s unless Heavy Tanks.
  • Air Support: max 1 per ATK
  • HQ: max 1 per ATK
  • Elite Combined Arms: Elite Mech and Inf stacked together
  • Low Quality Units: all contributed units are Low

11. Determined Defense (DD)

  • May attempt DD to cancel the Retreat portions.
  • Any Good Order, not HQs nor entrained, can be Lead Unit; Fort Unit if possible.
  • French Collapse: if Lead Unit is French and OOS at the moment of DD.
  • DD Air Support: German only. Max 1 per DD. Air Unit spent on Defensive Support can provide DD Air Support at no extra cost.
  • If DD removes the last step at a successful DD, the attacker may advance into the vacated hex but stop.

11.4. Desperate Defense

  • Allowed when [retreating force elimination / port hex & losing it force OOS after retreat]. Must choose either DD or Desperate Defense before any roll.
  • Restriction: [DS / DR4] cannot perform.
  • Procedure: Attempt DD as usual manner. If Fail, apply “-/-1”, and try again until [retreat is cancelled / all defending units lost].
  • The DD Air Support DRM is applied to every roll. Lead Unit may be selected for each attempt.

12. Retreats

  • Retreating through friendly units do not disturb non-retreating units.
  • Direction: In the following priority, if possible, 1) No same hex twice, no zig-zag, 2) Not causing step loss, 3) X hexes away, lose steps for unfulfilled, 4) in Supply position.
  • Over-stacking: May end retreat over-stacked, but should be resolved the next owning player’s Movement Phase end; if force to retreat again in the same phase, elimininated.
  • Through EZOC does not always cause step loss.
  • Elimination if [off map / into prohibited / across ZBond / into two consecutive EZOC / into one uncontested EZOC other than the first retreat hex / ending in EZOC; May retreat exactly one more hex to avoid eliminiation; not allowed for after two consecutive EZOC / across Major River even if bridge].
  • Early Stopping without Penalty if [retreated 1+ hexes & not ending in EZOC unless negated & into friendly City or Fortified (Allied only)]; Does not affect AAC result, Disruption, nor Full Retreat.

13. Distruption

  • Disrupted when retreat 1-3.
  • Exert ZOC, NO ZBond.
  • TAC MOV only.
  • May not attack.
  • DEF STR halved (earn Tank Shift).
  • HQ may not provide Shift nor Ready.
  • Retain TQ, ZOC, and Tank Shift.
  • Other units may enter or pass through friendly Disrupted units without becoming Disrupted.

13. Full Retreat

  • when [DR4+ / disrupted & retreat again / voluntary during friendly Movement Phase; from disrupted or GQG] (Cannot voluntarily Full Retreat on GT1, Sickle Cut)
  • NO ZOC, No ZBond.
  • Full MA; Extended Movement possible, no +2MP for leaving EZOC, may not enter EZOC unless negated.
  • May not ATK.
  • Cannot contribute to Defense.
  • HQ may not provide Shift nor Ready.
  • Must retreat 2 hexes if adj by enemy units unless negated; the enemy may then continue moving/advancing/retreating.

13. Recovery

  • dr if EZOC, automatic otherwise.
  • Full Retreat may choose not to recover.

14. AAC

  • Units stacked together but not participating the attack can AAC together.
  • Except Limited Advance, may advance in any direction. No need to enter the vacated hex
  • Stop upon entering EZOC (even if negated) unless the vacated.
  • Stop upon crossing a Major River even if bridged; may only cross during the first hex of AAC.
  • Cannot enter Restricted nor prohibited.

15. Breakthrough (BRK) Combat

  • Procedure: 1) Declare one BRK Group , 2) conduct AAC and BRK combat, 3) conduct AAC of the other units in attack
  • Breakthrough Group: With AAC eligible units. Apply Stacking Limit & Allied Cooperation. Can form in the vacated hex.
  • May DD Air Support if Air Unit already in the hex.
  • If fails to vacate or force to retreat, end the AAC.
  • If succeess, continue the AAC from the [vacated (at no extra cost) / launched] hex; if ends in the vacated while EZOC, end or try another to free itself.
  • Can drop off units from the BRK Group. The dropped end the AAC.
  • Unlike retreats from regular Combat, units that have already retreated may defend against BRK combat and are not eliminated if forced to retreat again; if overstacked, the defender choose some not to contribute.

16. Fortfications

  • Fort Unit: eliminated if forced to retreat
  • German never benefit from Allied Fortified Hexes.

17. Allied HQs

  • To provide HQ Shift, the HQ must 7- hexes to participating friendly unit and have the same nationality of one of the participating units.
  • May TAC MOV maintaining Ready or consider it as Mech by Used.
  • Adj alone to enemy unit, retreat 2 hexes & Disrupted; Full Retreat if already Disrupted.
  • Ready if Allied Initial Phase & Good Order & In Supply to W, S, SE, or E Entry Hex.
  • No dr if IS; instead eliminate if OOS & no LOC to Allied non-HQ units at Allied Supply Phase end.

18. Supply

  • Check during owning player’s Supply Phase; In Supply if trace LOC. OOS otherwise.
  • Supply Source: See Scenario rules. Allied tracing three friendly controlled City is In Supply.
  • Line of Supply: Consist of 5- hexes overland portion, and then, may use unlimited length of road portions; overland Wood Rough and Marsh costs 2 hexes.
    • Overland Portion: prohibited entering [two consecutive EZOC / an uncontested EZOC / enemy controlled City]; may negated.
    • Road Portion: prohibited entering [EZOC / enemy controlled City]; may negated.
  • OOS Effects: Only TAC MOV. ATT STR havled. AAC 2- hexes. No replacements.
    • Note. OOS allows ZOC, TQ, Tank Shift, full DEF STR.

18.5. Isolation Attrition

  • dr individually if red OOS except [HQ / not adj to enemy / overland LOC to any length / Fort Units if stacked or adj to friendly non-Fort unit]
  • Fort itself provides +2 DRM.
  • May lose the last step.

19. Reinforcements

  • Entry Hex is not playable. Reinforcements may not remain in those hexes or attack from them. Closed if Good Order enemy exit the Entry hex.
  • During Movement Phase through Entry paying TEC cost of the first hex entered; May reinforce on ZOC but stop; delayed to the following turn if entry impossible.
  • May use full MA on arrival.
  • Allied (Sickle Cut): Random draw as per TRT. See Reinforcement areas on the counters. Place one per hex if possible; two if impossible. May delay to enter via adj Entry area by Strategic Rail MOV; Put Rail MOV marker.
  • German: Max one division on each Entry Hex per turn; May 2 or 3 units constitute a division, not applicable to indep battalion (may enter with a division). Area A (north) is available once some enters on the area.

20. Special Rules

  • Recon Unit: Yellow DEF STR. Ignores step loss of [D1 / DS] if attacked alone in a hex.
  • Rommel: For each time ATK, DEF, BRK, and lead DD, allowed one re-roll each.
  • Montgomery: If DEF & Good Order, allowed one re-roll for each CRT and DD; Rommel decide rerolling first if both present.
  • Disengagement: Good Order attacker may attempt Disengagement attack once during any point of friendly Combat Phase. dr for each unit or group of units (owner’s choice). Retreat 2- hexes in any direction if successful. DIS but recover.

21. Sickle Cut Scenario

  • Supply Source: A, B, C, and D Entry for Axis; other Entry for Allied
  • The Dyle Line: Under constructions are finished at GT1 Allied Movement Phase end if 1+ Inf start there and stay there. Act as Fortified Hex. Remove if [boxed H on DD / unit in the hex are eliminated or force to retreat / no friendly units owning player’s Supply Phase end].
  • German Mass Air Strike: Max 3 Air in a single combat on GT1
  • Additional Turn (Optional): Upon agreement at game start, on GT10, player with 5+VP pay 3VP to extend the game one additional turn.

21.2. GQG (Grand Quartier General)

  • Effects: To slow down and prevent ATK.
    • Must stacked together; not split.
    • May only TAC MOV.
    • May not ATK or Disengagement.
    • May not stop voluntarily in a hex containing GQG; allowed for a while upon inevitable situation of Retreats, resolve ASAP.
    • At any Phase end, suffer GQG if stacked with; but can pass without affected.
  • Abilities: May enter EZOC. Retain ZOC, TQ, Tank Shift, Full DEF, and Lead DD.
  • Units Exempt: HQ and 4th DCR (de Gaulle). Free from, may freely exit and pass through GQG.

21.2.4. The GQG Phase

  • On GT1, place six GQG
  • GT2+, place from GQG Holding Box.
  • One per hex, on any Allied stack with 1+ French (even if under Train)
  • two dr to return 2-
  • Return to Holding Box: If GQG stack [eliminated / Disrupted / Full Retreat]; Allied may not voluntarily Full Retreat GQG units.
  • Scheduled Removal: GT3+, before placement during GQG Phase, remove one in the numerical order per turn from play (1→2→…).

21.3. von Rundstedt’s Halt Order

  • When: Once per game, any turn GT5+, during Allied Supply Phase.
  • How: Place 6- Halt markers on hexes containing 1+ German Mech; selected must be West further than all German non-Mech divisions. 3- hexes from German non-Mech cannot be selected.
  • After placement, two dr to remove the markers matching the each dr.
  • Effect: Same as GQG. Last for one German turn; remove after.

21.7. Victory Condition

Allied

  • 10VP if [X / N] and [S / W] have railroad connection; must be free from German unit or EZOC.
  • 5VP if not above, but can trace LOS from [X / N] to [S / W]; 5- overland & road to [S / W].
  • 1VP per eli multi-step German unit (3-step’s REM should be eliminated to count); Single-step battalion worth no VP.
  • 1VP per multi-step German Mech with no LOC of anylength

German

  • 10VP if Allied rail connection fails.
  • 1VP for each Allied Entry closed; 2VP for S/SE near Reim; to count, must LOS at the game end.
  • 1VP per German exited [X]; must trace LOS at the instant of exit.
